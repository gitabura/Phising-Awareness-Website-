How to Identify a Phishing Attack

Phishing messages often try to create panic or excitement so that you act without thinking. Always verify before clicking any link or sharing personal information.

📧 Check the Sender

Verify the sender's email address carefully. Attackers often use addresses that look similar to real ones.

🔗 Check Links

Hover over or inspect links before opening them. Do not click suspicious or shortened links.

⚠️ Beware of Urgent Messages

Messages claiming your account will be closed immediately are a common phishing tactic.

🔒 Never Share Passwords

Legitimate companies do not ask for your password or OTP by email, phone, or SMS.
